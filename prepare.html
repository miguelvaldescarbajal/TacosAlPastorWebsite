    • How To Prepare Tacos al Pastor

    In this step-by-step tutorial, we'll reveal the secrets behind crafting the perfect Tacos al Pastor right in your own kitchen. From marinating the succulent pork to mastering the art of grilling, get ready to embark on an epicurean adventure that will leave your taste buds singing. Join us as we uncover the magic of this beloved Mexican street food classic and learn how to recreate it with authenticity and flair. Let's roll up our sleeves and get cooking!

    Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce
  • 1 tablespoon achiote paste
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Pineapple slices (for serving)
  • Chopped onions and cilantro (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

    • Instructions

  • In a blender, combine the pineapple juice, minced garlic, chipotle peppers, achiote paste, dried oregano, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth to create the marinade.
  • Place the thinly sliced pork shoulder in a large bowl or resealable plastic bag. Pour the marinade over the pork, making sure it's evenly coated. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.
  • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat or preheat your oven broiler.
  • If using a grill, thread the marinated pork slices onto skewers. If using an oven broiler, arrange the pork slices on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil.
  • Grill the pork skewers or broil the pork slices for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and slightly charred on the edges.
  • While the pork is cooking, warm the corn tortillas on a skillet or griddle until soft and pliable.
  • Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the grill or oven and let it rest for a few minutes. Then, thinly slice or shred the pork into bite-sized pieces.
  • To assemble the tacos, place some of the cooked pork onto each warm corn tortilla. Top with a slice of grilled pineapple, and garnish with chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.
  • Serve the Tacos al Pastor immediately, accompanied by extra lime wedges and your favorite salsa or hot sauce on the side.
